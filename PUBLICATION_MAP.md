# MQGT-SCF Publication Map

This document explains where to find different versions and components of the MQGT-SCF research program.

## Article Channels

**Core Article (Operational Constraints):**
- **arXiv:** Submitted (awaiting endorsement)
- **viXra:** Submitted
- **Location:** `papers/unified_ToE/` in this repository

These channels host the concise, article-scale presentation of the framework with experimental constraints.

## Canonical Archive

**Zenodo (DOI):** [https://zenodo.org/records/18050570](https://zenodo.org/records/18050570)

- Versioned, persistent identifier
- Complete research corpus with metadata
- Canonical reference for citation
- Includes all supporting materials

## Library Mirror

**Internet Archive:** [https://archive.org/details/mqgt-scf-research-collection-full](https://archive.org/details/mqgt-scf-research-collection-full)

- Public library mirror for long-term access
- Full research collection (~4,850 pages)
- Preserved as research archive (not formatted as journal article)
- Accessible without institutional access

## Structured Edition

**Volumes 0–III:**

- **Volume 0:** Overview & Roadmap - [`docs/MQGT_SCF_Volume_0_Overview.pdf`](docs/MQGT_SCF_Volume_0_Overview.pdf)
  - Navigational entry point
  - Volume map and reading pathways
  - Structural index

- **Volume I:** Foundations & Core Formalism (~2,170 pages)
- **Volume II:** Proofs, Constraints, Extensions & Computation (~1,448 pages)
- **Volume III:** Interpretation, Applications & AI (~1,206 pages)

The structured volumes are maintained locally and accessible through Volume 0. The complete corpus is preserved in Zenodo and Internet Archive.

## Where to Go for What

- **Quick overview:** Start with Volume 0 (this repository)
- **Core article:** arXiv/viXra or `papers/unified_ToE/`
- **Full corpus (canonical):** Zenodo DOI
- **Full corpus (library access):** Internet Archive
- **Structured reading:** Volume 0 → Volumes I–III
- **Code and reproducibility:** This repository (`code/`, `notebooks/`)

## Citation

For citation of the research program, use the Zenodo DOI as the canonical reference. For citation of specific results, refer to the corresponding article or volume as appropriate.
